Chansong Kim

b.1988 / Based in South Korea and France

Chansong Kim captures moments when the solidity of existence dissolves into fluidity. Her long-standing inquiry into the 'body' focuses on the instant when her once-familiar form becomes suddenly estranged, perceived instead through a material, tactile sensibility. By fragmenting and magnifying parts of the body or obscuring the face, her works blur the boundary between the viewer's fixed preconceptions and the subject itself.

More recently, she has shifted from anonymous bodies to the surface of 'skin,' exploring sensorially the relationship between interior and exterior. For Kim, skin is both boundary and point of contact - a mutable surface that records traces of time and encounter. The chance recognition of a bead of sweat on his arm revealed the skin's autonomous vitality, breathing and interacting with the outside world prior to conscious awareness, provoking an acute existential awareness. In her practice, skin is not merely an outline of form but a mediator of sensation, a threshold between within and without.
